### Analysis
While the automated analysis can bring pertinent risk prediction on various segments, it is sometimes expected to draw out manual analysis and pass on information to business to identify edge cases too for reinvent strategies.

## Overview
This document establishes standard operating procedures for customer accounts flagged for manual review following Recency, Frequency, and Monetary (RFM) segmentation. The objective is to proactively mitigate churn risks, capture retention opportunities, and optimize high-value customer lifetime value (LTV).

---

## 1. High-Value Champions at Risk

### RFM Profile & Segment
* **Pattern:** High Recency (Long time since last purchase) | High Frequency | High Monetary
* **Segment:** At-Risk VIPs / Top Tier

### Risk Assessment
These are historically your most valuable assets. High revenue generation followed by a sudden cessation of activity typically signals a critical service failure, competitor poaching, or a major shift in customer needs.

### Manual Review Checklist
* [ ] Verify exact days since last interaction/purchase.
* [ ] Audit support history for unresolved complaints or negative CSAT scores.
* [ ] Review billing logs for failed payments or expired payment methods.
* [ ] Analyze historical purchase cycles to detect anomalies.

### Strategic Interventions
* **Direct Outreach:** Initiate a personal retention call or white-glove email from an Account Manager.
* **Premium Incentives:** Offer exclusive, high-tier loyalty rewards or VIP-specific discounts.
* **Bespoke Curation:** Provide personalized product recommendations based on past high-value behavior.

---

## 2. Frequent Buyers with Declining Spend

### RFM Profile & Segment
* **Pattern:** Increasing Recency (Gradually slowing down) | High Frequency | Medium-to-Low Monetary
* **Segment:** Potential Churn / Value Degradation

### Risk Assessment
The customer remains active, but their wallet share is shrinking. This pattern usually points to partial churn—where a customer begins shifting their primary volume to a competitor.

### Manual Review Checklist
* [ ] Map the spending trajectory over the last 3, 6, and 12 months.
* [ ] Identify if specific product categories have dropped off the customer's radar.
* [ ] Check for a declining average order value (AOV) or reduced items-per-order.

### Strategic Interventions
* **Cross-Selling:** Deploy targeted cross-selling campaigns featuring complementary categories.
* **Average Order Value (AOV) Boosters:** Introduce volume-based bundle offers (e.g., "Spend $X, Save $Y").
* **Re-engagement:** Launch discovery campaigns for newly released product lines.

---

## 3. One-and-Done New Customers

### RFM Profile & Segment
* **Pattern:** High Recency (Recent initial purchase) | Low Frequency | Low Monetary
* **Segment:** Cold New Casuals

### Risk Assessment
The customer made an initial purchase but failed to cross the threshold into habituation. This indicates a potential breakdown in the onboarding experience or unfulfilled initial expectations.

### Manual Review Checklist
* [ ] Audit the post-purchase onboarding email sequence engagement.
* [ ] Check delivery tracking for delays, damages, or fulfillment errors.
* [ ] Review any early satisfaction surveys or product returns.

### Strategic Interventions
* **Nurture Sequences:** Trigger an optimized welcome/education campaign highlighting product utility.
* **Conversion Incentives:** Offer a high-converting "second purchase" discount code.
* **Feedback Loops:** Send a quick, low-friction survey to identify barriers to entry.

---

## 4. Sleeping Loyalists

### RFM Profile & Segment
* **Pattern:** Low Recency (Long-term inactive) | High Frequency | Medium-to-High Monetary
* **Segment:** Dormant Core Customers

### Risk Assessment
These are historically loyal customers who have fully drifted away. Because they already know and trust the brand, they present a high win-back probability if targeted correctly.

### Manual Review Checklist
* [ ] Calculate the breach of standard purchase gap (e.g., missed their usual 30-day cycle).
* [ ] Review historical behavior to identify if their dormancy aligns with seasonal trends.
* [ ] Audit past communications to see if they opted out of marketing.

### Strategic Interventions
* **Win-Back Campaigns:** Execute "We Miss You" reactivation campaigns with strong, time-sensitive hooks.
* **Loyalty Renewal:** Offer account reactivation perks or point boosts.
* **Direct Feedback:** Request a brief review of why they stopped buying to adjust internal processes.

---

## 5. Low-Value Inactive Accounts

### RFM Profile & Segment
* **Pattern:** Low Recency | Low Frequency | Low Monetary
* **Segment:** Lost / Churned Casuals

### Risk Assessment
Low engagement paired with minimal revenue contribution. These accounts yield a poor return on investment (ROI) for manual or high-cost retention efforts.

### Manual Review Checklist
* [ ] Identify the original acquisition channel (to optimize future marketing spend away from low-LTV channels).
* [ ] Ensure Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) vs. LTV math justifies any automated outreach.

### Strategic Interventions
* **Low-Touch Automation:** Funnel into automated, low-cost email reactivation sequences.
* **Suppression List:** Exclude from expensive direct mail, SMS, or ad-retargeting campaigns.

## Action Priority Matrix

Review queues should be cleared based on the following prioritization framework to maximize revenue preservation:

| Priority | Risk Profile | Customer Segment | Core Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High** | High-Value Churn Risk | At-Risk VIPs / Champions | Immediate, personalized high-touch intervention |
| **Medium** | Declining Spend / Sleeping | Potential Churn & Dormant Loyalists | Targeted win-back & value-optimization campaigns |
| **Medium** | One-and-Done Risks | New Customers | Automated engagement & onboarding correction |
| **Low** | Low-Value Inactive | Lost Casuals | Low-cost, fully automated marketing tracks |
